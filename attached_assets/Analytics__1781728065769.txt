Analytics and Operations Engine (AOE) — Full Detailed Specification
1. Purpose
The Analytics and Operations Engine is the platform subsystem responsible for transforming raw product, trust, traffic, fraud, billing, and system telemetry into actionable business intelligence, operational insights, and automated workflow support.
Its primary job is to answer:
	1.	What is happening across the platform right now?
	2.	Why is it happening?
	3.	Where are the risks, bottlenecks, and opportunities?
	4.	What operational action should be taken next?
Unlike a simple reporting layer, the Analytics and Operations Engine is both:
	•	Analytical, in that it aggregates, measures, slices, and forecasts platform behavior.
	•	Operational, in that it powers alerts, queues, investigations, playbooks, SLA monitoring, and executive visibility.
 
⸻
 
2. Core Objectives
The engine should:
	•	Provide real-time and historical analytics across all PoH systems
	•	Surface operational health, performance, and anomaly insights
	•	Track product, trust, fraud, revenue, and traffic KPIs in one place
	•	Detect trends, regressions, spikes, and irregularities
	•	Support dashboards, alerts, exports, and executive reporting
	•	Enable team workflows for analysts, operators, and customer success
	•	Measure the effectiveness of trust and fraud decisions
	•	Provide forecasting, capacity planning, and business performance visibility
	•	Feed data back into decision engines and model improvement loops

5. Architecture Overview
The engine is composed of the following layers:
5.1 Data Ingestion Layer
Collects telemetry from:
	•	APIs
	•	SDKs
	•	event streams
	•	databases
	•	logs
	•	decision engines
	•	billing systems
	•	customer workflows
	•	support systems
	•	infrastructure monitoring
5.2 Normalization Layer
Standardizes timestamps, entities, dimensions, labels, and metric definitions.
5.3 Aggregation Layer
Builds hourly, daily, weekly, monthly, and tenant-specific aggregates.
5.4 Analytics Layer
Runs descriptive analytics, diagnostic analysis, trend detection, and forecasting.
5.5 Operations Layer
Drives monitoring, alerting, queues, assignments, and escalation workflows.
5.6 Reporting Layer
Produces dashboards, exports, scheduled reports, and executive summaries.
5.7 Feedback Layer
Feeding operational outcomes back into:
	•	policy tuning
	•	model retraining
	•	threshold tuning
	•	product prioritization

8. Scoring Model
8.1 Suggested Composite Weighting
A balanced model could weigh:
	•	Platform Analytics: 15%
	•	Product Analytics: 15%
	•	Trust Analytics: 15%
	•	Traffic Analytics: 10%
	•	Revenue Analytics: 15%
	•	Operations Analytics: 10%
	•	Customer Success Analytics: 10%
	•	Executive Analytics: 5%
	•	Forecasting Accuracy: 5%
Weights should be tenant- and audience-configurable.
8.2 Composite Formula
Analytics and Operations Health Score =
(Platform * 0.15) +
(Product * 0.15) +
(Trust * 0.15) +
(Traffic * 0.10) +
(Revenue * 0.15) +
(Operations * 0.10) +
(CustomerSuccess * 0.10) +
(Executive * 0.05) +
(ForecastQuality * 0.05)
8.3 Confidence Adjustment
The final score should be adjusted by:
	•	data freshness
	•	completeness
	•	source reliability
	•	consistency across systems
	•	anomaly levels

14. Processing Flow
Real-time flow
	1.	Source event arrives from platform or downstream engine
	2.	Event is normalized and timestamped
	3.	Metric stores and stream processors update aggregates
	4.	Anomaly detection evaluates deviations
	5.	Alerting engine evaluates thresholds
	6.	Queue items are created if needed
	7.	Dashboards refresh
	8.	Reports and notifications are emitted
Batch flow
	1.	Historical data is scanned
	2.	Aggregates are recomputed
	3.	Forecast models are updated
	4.	Cohort analysis is refreshed
	5.	Executive reports are produced
	6.	Baseline and threshold tuning occurs

17. Reliability and Fallback Behavior
If a downstream source is unavailable:
	•	use cached aggregates
	•	preserve previous baselines
	•	degrade forecasting confidence
	•	continue alerting where thresholds are still known
	•	mark metrics as stale instead of failing silently
Fallback modes:
	•	partial analytics
	•	stale-data mode
	•	batch-only recomputation
	•	queue-only operational mode
	•	manual operator override
