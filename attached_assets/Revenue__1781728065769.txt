Revenue Protection Engine (RPE) — Full Detailed Specification
1. Purpose
The Revenue Protection Engine is the platform subsystem responsible for detecting, preventing, scoring, and responding to threats that reduce realized revenue, distort financial performance, or create avoidable loss across the customer lifecycle.
Its primary job is to answer:
	1.	Is this revenue real, collectible, and durable?
	2.	Is this transaction, account, or customer likely to generate loss later?
	3.	Is there evidence of chargeback risk, refund abuse, promo abuse, subscription fraud, billing fraud, or revenue leakage?
	4.	What action should the platform take now to preserve revenue?
Revenue Protection is broader than fraud detection. It includes fraud, abuse, leakage, operational loss, and financial integrity monitoring.
 
⸻
 
2. Core Objectives
The engine should:
	•	Protect revenue from fraud, abuse, and misuse
	•	Detect chargeback and refund risk early
	•	Reduce failed payments, involuntary churn, and bad debt
	•	Identify promo abuse, coupon abuse, and incentive abuse
	•	Prevent subscription fraud and trial abuse
	•	Detect account takeover that leads to financial loss
	•	Surface revenue leakage across checkout, billing, support, and retention flows
	•	Support real-time decisioning and post-event investigations
	•	Provide explainable risk reasons for every score
	•	Feed downstream trust, fraud, finance, and retention systems

5. Architecture Overview
The engine is organized into modular subsystems.
5.1 Signal Collection Layer
Collects raw operational, transactional, behavioral, identity, and billing data.
5.2 Normalization Layer
Standardizes event formats, monetary values, currencies, payment outcomes, and lifecycle states.
5.3 Intelligence Modules
Each module specializes in one revenue risk domain:
	•	Billing Intelligence
	•	Transaction Intelligence
	•	Chargeback Intelligence
	•	Refund Intelligence
	•	Promo Abuse Intelligence
	•	Subscription Intelligence
	•	Retention Intelligence
	•	Account Risk Intelligence
	•	Behavioral Revenue Intelligence
	•	Graph Revenue Intelligence
5.4 Scoring Orchestration Layer
Combines module outputs into composite revenue protection scores.
5.5 Decision Layer
Transforms risk into operational actions.
5.6 Feedback and Learning Layer
Learns from disputes, reversals, reviews, cancellations, and collections outcomes.

8. Scoring Model
8.1 Suggested weighting model
A starting composite weighting structure:
	•	Transaction Intelligence: 20%
	•	Chargeback Intelligence: 15%
	•	Refund Abuse Intelligence: 10%
	•	Promo Abuse Intelligence: 10%
	•	Subscription Intelligence: 10%
	•	Billing Intelligence: 10%
	•	Retention Intelligence: 10%
	•	Account Risk Intelligence: 10%
	•	Behavioral Revenue Intelligence: 5%
	•	Graph Revenue Intelligence: 10%
Weights should be tenant-configurable and product-specific.
8.2 Composite formula
Revenue Protection Score =
(Transaction * 0.20) +
(Chargeback * 0.15) +
(Refund * 0.10) +
(Promo * 0.10) +
(Subscription * 0.10) +
(Billing * 0.10) +
(Retention * 0.10) +
(Account * 0.10) +
(Behavior * 0.05) +
(Graph * 0.10)
8.3 Confidence adjustment
Final score should be adjusted by confidence and signal completeness.
	•	High confidence: no penalty
	•	Medium confidence: slight downward adjustment
	•	Low confidence: conservative risk bias and review preference

9. Decision Engine
The decision engine maps revenue risk into operational action.
9.1 Action outcomes
	•	Approve
	•	Approve and monitor
	•	Delay fulfillment
	•	Step-up verification
	•	Hold refund
	•	Limit benefit issuance
	•	Require review
	•	Freeze account
	•	Deny refund
	•	Block transaction
	•	Escalate to finance or fraud operations
9.2 Example policy bands
	•	850–1000: Allow
	•	700–849: Allow and monitor
	•	550–699: Step-up or limit benefits
	•	400–549: Hold or review
	•	Below 400: Block or freeze depending on severity
9.3 Decision modifiers
	•	High-value order
	•	New account
	•	First transaction
	•	Sensitive product
	•	High-risk geography
	•	Payment method risk
	•	Known abuse cluster
	•	Prior refund abuse
	•	Enterprise policy settings
