Traffic Intelligence Engine (TIEg) — Full Detailed Specification
1. Purpose
The Traffic Intelligence Engine is the subsystem responsible for measuring the quality, legitimacy, intent, and risk of inbound traffic before it reaches conversion, signup, transaction, or API execution.
Its job is to answer:
	1.	Where did this traffic come from?
	2.	Does the source behave like real, valuable traffic?
	3.	Is the traffic organic, paid, engineered, or fraudulent?
	4.	Should this traffic be trusted, challenged, throttled, or blocked?
Traffic Intelligence is not just attribution. It is traffic trust evaluation.
 
⸻
 
2. Core Mission
The engine should:
	•	Determine traffic source legitimacy
	•	Detect bot traffic, synthetic clicks, fake leads, and referral abuse
	•	Measure campaign quality and source integrity
	•	Identify suspicious spikes, farms, and coordinated traffic manipulation
	•	Score traffic before and after conversion
	•	Distinguish high-intent human traffic from low-quality or malicious traffic
	•	Improve marketing, fraud prevention, and trust decisions
 
⸻
 
3. Primary Outputs
3.1 Traffic Trust Score
A normalized score representing the trustworthiness of the traffic source and session origin.
Recommended range:
0–1000
Interpretation example:
	•	900–1000: Elite traffic
	•	800–899: Trusted traffic
	•	700–799: Healthy traffic
	•	600–699: Acceptable traffic
	•	500–599: Mixed quality
	•	400–499: Suspicious traffic
	•	300–399: High-risk traffic
	•	0–299: Fraudulent or worthless traffic
 
⸻
 
3.2 Traffic Quality Score
Measures how likely traffic is to produce meaningful, legitimate engagement.
Recommended range:
0–100
 
⸻
 
3.3 Source Integrity Score
Measures whether the claimed source channel is credible and consistent with observed behavior.
Examples:
	•	Organic search that behaves like organic search
	•	Paid social that actually matches the campaign
	•	Referral traffic that aligns with the referring site
	•	Direct traffic that is actually direct
Recommended range:
0–100
 
⸻
 
3.4 Campaign Integrity Score
Measures whether a campaign is producing valid traffic or manipulated activity.
Recommended range:
0–100
 
⸻
 
3.5 Traffic Fraud Score
Measures probability that traffic is synthetic, manipulated, incentivized, or fraudulent.
Recommended range:
0–100
 
⸻
 
3.6 Traffic Confidence Score
Confidence in the engine’s classification based on signal completeness and source certainty.
Recommended range:
0–100
 
⸻
 
3.7 Traffic Risk Tier
Categorical label used by policy and enforcement systems.
	•	Low
	•	Medium
	•	Elevated
	•	High
	•	Critical
 
⸻
 
4. System Role in PoH Intelligence
The Traffic Intelligence Engine sits between acquisition and trust decisioning.
It helps answer whether a user session should be considered:
	•	Real and valuable
	•	Real but low intent
	•	Artificially boosted
	•	Manipulated by fraud
	•	Bot-driven
	•	Incentivized or low-quality
	•	Coordinated through a traffic ring
This engine feeds downstream modules like:
	•	Trust Intelligence Engine
	•	Human Intelligence Engine
	•	Fraud Intelligence Engine
	•	Decision Engine
	•	Marketing attribution and analytics

5. Traffic Intelligence Architecture
5.1 Ingestion Layer
Collects traffic-related events from:
	•	Page loads
	•	Landing pages
	•	Referrer headers
	•	UTM parameters
	•	Campaign IDs
	•	Ad clicks
	•	API requests
	•	App installs
	•	In-app referrals
	•	Conversion funnels
	•	Postback events
	•	Server-side logs
5.2 Normalization Layer
Standardizes source and campaign fields.
Examples:
	•	Normalize channel labels
	•	Resolve missing UTM values
	•	Infer source from patterns
	•	Deduplicate duplicated click events
	•	Clean malformed referrers
	•	Canonicalize domains and campaign identifiers
5.3 Intelligence Layer
Analyzes traffic patterns across:
	•	Source
	•	Session
	•	Geography
	•	Device
	•	Behavior
	•	Timing
	•	Conversion path
	•	Historical source reputation
5.4 Scoring Layer
Produces source, campaign, and traffic quality outputs.
5.5 Decision Layer
Applies policies such as:
	•	Accept
	•	Monitor
	•	De-prioritize
	•	Rate limit
	•	Challenge
	•	Exclude from attribution
	•	Flag for review
	•	Block

6.1 Source Intelligence
This domain evaluates the declared origin of traffic.
Source types

	•	Organic search
	•	Paid search
	•	Paid social
	•	Organic social
	•	Referral
	•	Email
	•	Affiliate
	•	Display
	•	Push
	•	SMS

	•	Partner
	•	Unknown
Signals

6.2 Campaign Intelligence
Evaluates whether a marketing campaign is producing valid traffic.
Signals

	•	UTM pattern integrity

	•	Device and geo consistency

6.8 Device and Network Cross-Validation

Red flags

	•	Device class mismatch with campaign audience
	•	Claimed organic traffic that behaves like automation

8. Scoring Model
8.1 Recommended weighting model
A balanced scoring structure could be:
	•	Source Intelligence: 15%
	•	Campaign Intelligence: 15%
	•	Engagement Intelligence: 15%
	•	Conversion Integrity: 15%
	•	Referral Intelligence: 10%
	•	Geographic Intelligence: 10%
	•	Temporal Intelligence: 10%
	•	Device/Network Cross-Validation: 10%
Weights should be configurable by tenant and use case.
 
⸻
 
8.2 Composite formula
Traffic Trust Score =
(Source Intelligence * 0.15) +
(Campaign Intelligence * 0.15) +
(Engagement Intelligence * 0.15) +
(Conversion Integrity * 0.15) +
(Referral Intelligence * 0.10) +
(Geographic Intelligence * 0.10) +
(Temporal Intelligence * 0.10) +
(Device/Network Cross-Validation * 0.10)
A separate fraud-adjusted version may apply penalties for confirmed manipulation:
Final Traffic Trust = Base Trust - Fraud Penalties + Confidence Adjustments
 
⸻
 
9. Fraud Detection Logic
The Traffic Intelligence Engine should detect:
9.1 Bot traffic
Indicators:
	•	Very low engagement
	•	Mechanical timing
	•	Repeated session patterns
	•	Headless browser signatures
	•	High volume from one infrastructure source
9.2 Click fraud
Indicators:
	•	Click bursts without downstream engagement
	•	Invalid or repeated click chains
	•	Repeated source clusters
	•	Conversion-free click amplification
9.3 Lead fraud
Indicators:
	•	Fake names, disposable emails, VOIP numbers
	•	Duplicate submissions
	•	Automated form completion
	•	Suspiciously high lead generation from one source
9.4 Referral fraud
Indicators:
	•	Artificial referral loops
	•	Self-referral structures
	•	Partner traffic with no real intent
	•	Misleading source labels
9.5 Incentive abuse
Indicators:
	•	Traffic designed only to earn rewards
	•	Ultra-short engagement
	•	Low retention
	•	Repeated account creation patterns
9.6 Traffic farming
Indicators:
	•	Large repeated patterns from low-quality networks
	•	Clustered device fingerprints
	•	Synchronized timing
	•	Shared infrastructure among many accounts

10. Decision Engine
Traffic decisions should be distinct from user trust decisions.
10.1 Traffic actions
	•	Allow
	•	Allow and monitor
	•	De-prioritize in attribution
	•	Discount in analytics
	•	Challenge
	•	Rate limit
	•	Quarantine
	•	Exclude from reports
	•	Block source
	•	Escalate for review
10.2 Example policy bands
	•	850–1000: Accept and attribute fully
	•	700–849: Accept, monitor, and attribute normally
	•	550–699: Attribute cautiously; lower confidence
	•	400–549: Discount attribution; flag for review
	•	Below 400: Exclude or block depending on severity
10.3 Decision modifiers
	•	New campaign
	•	Sensitive geography
	•	High-value conversion
	•	Known partner
	•	Unknown source
	•	Tenant risk tolerance
	•	Historical source reputation
	•	Prior abuse history

  },
  "signals": {
    "geo": {},
    "device": {},
    "network": {},
    "engagement": {},

  }

15. Reliability and Fallbacks
When key attribution or source data is missing:
	•	Use partial scoring
	•	Infer source from behavior and network signals
	•	Apply conservative confidence penalties
	•	Preserve the event for later recomputation
	•	Avoid hard-blocking without sufficient evidence unless fraud confidence is high
Fallback modes:
	•	Rule-only
	•	Partial-model
	•	Cached reputation
	•	Delayed recomputation
	•	Manual review mode
 
⸻
 
16. Security and Privacy Requirements
Security
	•	Encrypt in transit and at rest
	•	Authenticate all API calls
	•	Sign or verify event payloads where possible
	•	Prevent replay of traffic events
	•	Rate limit abusive scoring requests
	•	Audit all policy decisions
Privacy
	•	Minimize collection of personally identifying traffic data
	•	Hash identifiers when possible
	•	Apply retention windows
	•	Support deletion and export policies
	•	Separate raw traffic logs from derived intelligence

It gives PoH Intelligence the ability to distinguish real audience demand from fabricated, manipulated, or low-value traffic before that traffic contaminates analytics, attribution, fraud systems, or trust scores.
